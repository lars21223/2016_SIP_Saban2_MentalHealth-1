Mental Disorders

Many people tend to ridicule the severity of mental illnesses and pass them off as a phase that will go away after given some time alone. However, there are serious symptoms attached to each disorder that should not be overlooked. Mental disorders involve a range of long term symptoms that occur spontaneously. If ignored, these recurring affects can negatively impact the individual mentally, emotionally, and eventually physically. Thus, we have provided information on several disorders as well as some of the common symptoms attached in order to help address these obstacles you regularly face as well as provide a basis to work to a more comfortable state and manage your anxiety!

Anxiety Disorders

Panic Disorder
- an uncontrollable fear that strikes without reason or warning. Characterized by recurring panic attacks, causing a series of intense episodes of exteme anxiety.
  • Fast heartbeat
  • Chest or stomach pain
  • Breathing difficulty
  • Weakness or dizziness
  • Feeling hot or a cold chill
  • Tingly or numb hands

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder
- unreasonable thoughts and fears (obsessions) that lead to repetitive behaviors (compulsions). An individual can have one or the other or both.
  • overwhelming urge to repeat certain rituals/behaviors
    1. ex: washing hands, cleaning, checking things
  • frequent/upsetting thoughts
    1. ex: fear of germs or fear of being hurt

Post Traumatic Stress Disorder
- when an individual is still negatively affected by past shocking, scary or dangerous event. Feeling stressed or frightened even when not in danger.
  • Flashbacks, or feeling like the event is happening again
  • Trouble sleeping/nightmares
  • Feeling alone, worried, guilty, or sad
  • Angry outbursts

Phobias
- overwhelming and unreasonable fear of an object or situation that poses little danger but provokes anxiety and avoidance.
  • Panic and fear
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Shortness of breath
  • Trembling
  • Strong desire to get away
  • Rapid speech or inability to speak

Social Anxiety
- uncontrollable fear of social situations. Anxiety and self-consciousness arise from a fear of being closely watched, judged, and criticized by others.
  • Worrying about embarrassing or humiliating yourself
  • Having anxiety in anticipation of a feared activity or event
  • Intense fear of interacting or talking with strangers
  • Concern that you'll offend someone
  • Fast heartbeat
  • Upset stomach or nausea
  • dizziness or lightheadedness

Depression

Common Symptoms
  • Feeling sad or "empty"
  • Loss of interest in favorite activites
  • Overeating, or not wanting to eat at all
  • Not being able to sleep, or sleeping too much
  • Feeling very tired
  • Feeling hopeless, irritable, anxious, or guilty
  • Aches or pains, headaches, cramps, or digestive problems
  • Thoughts of death or suicide
  • Difficulty concentrating, remembering, making decisions

Types of Depression
  • Major depression:
    1. Interferes with the ability to enjoy everyday life
    2. Can occur once or more in a person's lifetime
  • Persistent depressive disorder:
    1. Depressed mood that lasts for 2 years
    2. Episodes of major depression + less severe symptoms.
  • Psychotic depression:
    1. Severe depression and some form of psychosis
    2. Experiences delusions and hallucinations
  • Postpartum depression:
    1. Hormonal and physical changes
    2. Due to the new responsibility of caring for a newborn
  • Seasonal affective disorder:
    1. Onset of depression during months with less natural sunlight
    2. It generally lifts during the spring and summer

Bipolar Disorder

Abrupt Mood Changes
  • Frequently/quickly go from "up" and "down"
  • "Up": mania; "down": depression
Manic Phase
  • Increased activity levels
  • Feel "jumpy" or "weird"
  • Talk really fast about a lot of different things
  • Agitated, irritable or "touchy"
  • Feels like their thoughts are going very fast
  • Think they can do a lot of things at once
Depressive Phase
  • Feel very sad, down, empty, or hopeless
  • Have very little energy
  • Have trouble sleeping (sleep too little or too much)
  • Have trouble concentrating
  • Forget things a lot
  • Eat too much or too little
  • Think about death or suicide
